📊 Unemployment Rate Analysis in India during COVID-19
📌 Project Overview
    This project performs an **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)** on the unemployment rate in India, focusing on the impact of the **COVID-19 pandemic and nationwide lockdowns** in 2020. The dataset includes estimated unemployment rates, employed populations, and labor participation rates across various states.
🛠️ Key Steps & Methodology
    1. Data Cleaning & Preprocessing: Handled missing values, stripped extra spaces from column names, and converted dates to datetime format.
    2. Exploratory Data Analysis: (EDA) Analyzed state-wise unemployment trends and identified the most affected regions.
    3. Time-Series Analysis: Visualized unemployment rate fluctuations over time to observe the impact of lockdowns.
    4. Pre vs Post COVID Comparison: Evaluated average unemployment rates before and after March 2020.
    5. Correlation Analysis: Generated heatmaps to understand inter-variable relationships.

---
💡 Key Observations & Insights
      COVID Spike: A severe surge in unemployment occurred between **April 2020 and June 2020** due to strict nationwide lockdowns.
      Worst Affected States: States like **Haryana, Tripura, Delhi, and Puducherry** experienced peak unemployment rates.
      Pre vs Post Lockdown: The overall average unemployment rate rose significantly from **9.23% (Pre-COVID)** to **12.96% (Post-COVID)**.
      Correlation: A negative correlation (-0.25) exists between the Unemployment Rate and Employed population.
